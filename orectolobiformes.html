The Orectolobiformes is an order of sharks often referred to as carpet sharks, which includes a variety of species with unique characteristics. These sharks are typically bottom-dwellers, with flattened bodies and wide heads. Many of them are slow-moving and have distinctive patterns or markings on their bodies that resemble carpet-like designs, hence their name.

Notable Species in the Orectolobiformes Order:

Whale Shark

The Whale Shark (Rhincodon typus) is the largest known species of shark and also the largest living fish. It is a filter feeder, consuming plankton and small fish by swimming with its mouth open. Despite their enormous size, Whale Sharks are gentle giants, often found in tropical waters.

Zebra Shark

The Zebra Shark (Stegostoma fasciatum) is another well-known species in the Orectolobiformes family. It is named for its distinctive striped pattern, which fades as the shark matures. Zebra Sharks are found in shallow waters, often resting on the seafloor during the day.

Other Species

In addition to the Whale Shark and Zebra Shark, the Orectolobiformes order includes species like the Nurse Shark and the Bamboo Shark. These sharks are typically bottom-dwellers, relying on their excellent camouflage and slow-moving nature to blend in with their surroundings.
