The Squatiniformes order of sharks is a distinctive group often referred to as Angel Sharks due to their flattened, wing-like bodies. These sharks are bottom-dwellers, usually found in shallow coastal waters, where their unique body shape helps them camouflage in the sand. Despite their angelic appearance, they are powerful predators, capable of ambushing prey with speed and precision.

Notable Species in the Squatiniformes Order:

Common Angel Shark (Squatina squatina)

The Common Angel Shark is the most well-known species in the Squatiniformes order. It is recognized for its flattened body, which is adapted to help it blend into the seafloor. Angel Sharks are ambush predators, waiting for unsuspecting prey to pass by before attacking with a rapid burst of speed.

Japanese Angel Shark (Squatina japonica)

The Japanese Angel Shark is native to the coastal waters around Japan and is similar to the Common Angel Shark in appearance and behavior. These sharks have been known to inhabit deeper waters than other angel sharks and are typically found at depths of around 100 meters.

Other Species

Besides the Common and Japanese Angel Sharks, the Squatiniformes order also includes species like the Sand Devil Shark and the Atlantic Angel Shark. These species share the same flattened body shape and ambush hunting style, often hiding in sandy or muddy ocean floors where they wait for prey to approach.
