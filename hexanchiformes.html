The Hexanchiformes are an ancient order of sharks that have a lineage dating back over 300 million years. Known for their unique characteristics, including having six or seven gill slits, these sharks are among the most primitive living species of sharks. They are typically found in deep waters and are rarely seen by humans.

Notable Species in the Hexanchiformes Order:

Bluntnose Sixgill Shark

The Bluntnose Sixgill Shark (Hexanchus griseus) is one of the most well-known species of this order. It is named for the six gill slits on each side of its body, which is a key characteristic of the Hexanchiformes. These sharks can grow up to 5 meters long and are typically found in deep ocean waters.

Sharpnose Sevengill Shark

The Sharpnose Sevengill Shark (Heptranchias perlo) is another member of the Hexanchiformes family. With seven gill slits on each side, this species is easily identifiable. It is often found in deeper waters but has been known to occasionally venture into shallower coastal areas.

Other Species

Besides the Bluntnose Sixgill Shark and Sharpnose Sevengill Shark, there are other species in the Hexanchiformes order, each with its own unique traits and habitats. These sharks are often deepwater dwellers, making them more elusive and mysterious in comparison to other shark species.
